But education isn't just about personal development.

It's effects are far wider and long term.

What has this got to do with you? (you might ask)

If you already know how to write code you may think education is of little interest to you.

If you're good you know dev involves learning and, when in position of responsibility, teaching others how your code works.

To be a programmer is to be a teacher and learner.

This doesn't just apply in a professional context.

To describe programming to children indicates you know your craft at a deep level:

  • Appreciate what to leave in or how much to take out
  • Clear mental models to accurately analogize and summarise
  • Explain youself in simple & understandable language that demonstrates your own clarity of thought
  • It's a sign of moral and professional value: you're putting something back into the community.
