What is it?

MicroPython is a re-implementation of Python 3 optimised for microcontrollers.

What does it do?

It brings the power and beauty of Python programming to highly constrained computing environments.

Why is it suddenly everywhere?

It facilitiates simple yet rapid development on embedded devices. It has powerful features. It uses a transferable skillset. It has an outstanding community.

How?

(The technical bit...)

  • lexer - characters to tokens
  • parser - take tokens and return parse tree
  • compiler - parse tree into executable stuff

MicroPython Coding Style

(Does not follow traditional software engineering practices)

  • optimise first;
  • creative solutions and tricks;
  • sacrifice clarity for smaller code;
  • sacrifice efficiency for smaller code;
  • use of goto not discouraged;
  • optimise to minimise stack usage;
  • decisions based on analysis.

What has education got to do with you..?

  • Part of our job as developers is to teach / mentor each other.
  • To describe programming so kids understand you indicates you know your craft at a deep level:
  • You know what to leave in and appreciate what should be left out of an explanation.
  • You have clear enough mental models of the concepts of programming that you can accurately analogize and summarise.
  • By explaining yourself in simple and easy to understand languages demonstrates your own clarity of thought.
  • Finally, it is unambiguously good to put something back into the wider Python community.
