20 Years

Wednesday 2nd August 2017 (9:00am)

Today is our 20th wedding anniversary. Such an auspicious occasion deserves celebrating.

Mary and I face the world together: we laugh with each other, we trust each other and we rely on each other. It is a great source of strength and comfort that the person I love most in the world thinks of me as the person they love most in the world. It's a virtuous circle of mutual love, support, friendship and affection. Since this is something only Mary and I share with each other, it makes it all the more precious.

Yet we find ourselves in a wonderful situation. Our bonds strengthen over time as our shared experience grows ~ for example, as parents bringing up our three children to be confident, independent and flourishing individuals.

How did this begin?

I first met Mary in the Donaldson Room in the library of the Royal College of Music. By chance, we were sat at the same table. I was with a buddy and she was with her friend, Ellen. They were playing "hangman" and instead of calling out the vowels to get the shape of the word to be guessed, Mary would say things like "X", "Q" or "W".

"You have absolutely no idea how to play hangman, do you?", I interrupted.

"Oh yes I do... I'm just playing it this way for fun."

Three hours later our friends had left us as we talked the afternoon away discussing science, philosophy and music. At the end Mary said, "I'm very sorry, but I'm late for a rehearsal. I'd much rather talk to you... what's your name?"

I'd never met anyone like her. That was it. In that moment. She was the one.

We were very fortunate to meet and marry at a young age. Consequently, it's likely that we have 40 years or more of marriage ahead of us.

So, here's to facing the future together and long may it last.

(In case you were wondering, we've agreed to hold a big party for all our friends and family on the occasion of our 25th anniversary: 2nd August, 2022.)
