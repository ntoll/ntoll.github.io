Then I remembered a distant memory from my childhood.

A MUD (Multi-User-Dungeon) is a multi-player text based adventure game. They were, and continue to be, very very popular.

  • Low-tech

LambdaMOO - a programmable textual world.

There are many varieties of MUD. In the 1990s, while at university I would play on MOO - Multi-user Object Oriented worlds.

The world was a collection of objects. Users could program the behaviour of the world as well as inhabit it.

A Rape in Cyberspace

MOO became very popular. Before all the toxic aspects of current social media platforms, such behaviours were manifested in these virtual textual worlds. Yet at the same time, these were progressive places and I made many friends all over the world at a time when such things were still viewed with incredulity.

What would a MUD/MOO written in 2020 look like?

Things I love about MUDs:

  • Tell, don't show. The user's imagination is paramount.
  • Playful or serious "games" that tell a story within a recognisable literary genre.
  • Very low overhead in technical terms.
  • Graphical games focus on hand/eye coordination, text based worlds put narrative, meaning and intent at the centre of their process.
  • While the reader may have to do more, the reward is far greater.
  • Literary worlds are more egalitarian and accessible - within the realm of a single developer.
