Part 4.
Culture And Code

  • Clearly it's not just about the theory of music.
  • Should it really be about the very narrow aspects of coding that deal with syntax, algorithms or computer science.
  • As WE all know - there is a coding culture to learn too: practices, processes and people-skills that allow us to collaboratively build the digital world together.

Thought experiment

What if we taught programming like we teach music?

  • How might we transmit the essentials of a coding culture?
  • Introduce John. Founded PyConUK 2007. EuroPython chair 2009-2010, lost his fight with cancer in 2015. An excellent engineer, formidable organiser and wonderful mentor.
  • John Pinner and I used Music for Youth as our template for the PyConUK education track.
  • Coding education isn't just explaining stuff.
  • We attempted to embody the sorts of behaviour, attitudes, skills, attention and knowledge we hoped to...

...inspire in our newest Pythonistas.

We wanted them to see this is a long, slow...

...and at times frustrating journey.

But it's a journey made possible because folks (no matter their age or level of coding) were encouraged to step up and support learners.

By taking these steps learners encountered and practiced what it is to be a Python coder...

...in an inclusive, supportive and diverse community.

Between 2012-2015, each year, we welcomed up to 50 teachers and over 100 kids to PyConUK.

We encouraged folks to sustain their learning journey, build on from what they had encountered at the conference, and step into our community.

(Just as the music mentors at Music for Youth do.)

And we were not the only ones to do this.
