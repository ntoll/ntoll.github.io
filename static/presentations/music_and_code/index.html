But before embarking on my talk, I want to find out about you, too.

Are there any musicians in the audience? Anyone consider themselves tone deaf? There's no such thing as tone deaf: we all know when a car is in the wrong gear, the difference between our cell phone's ringtone and that of another, and can recognize people from their voices (and even their mood too). Our aural capabilities are truly phenomenal.

Any learners in the room? How many of you are still coding with exactly the same set of tools and frameworks as 10, 5, or even 2 years go..? Change is constant and part of being a good developer is to always be learning. Right..?

Any teachers in the room? If you work in a school or educational establishment, thank you so much for the work that you do. Professional teachers usually never get thanked, are the butt of political point scoring and yet still embody a profession whose aim is to help people find and nurture their passion, potential and personality.

How many of you write documentation, pair program, present ideas or code for review in meetings or as part of your development process..? Teaching and mentoring is a core aspect of our job as developers.
