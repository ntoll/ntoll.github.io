Part 4.
Expression

Why is this so important..?

Code isn't written for computers, in the same way music isn't written for instruments.

We write code because we are human beings, imbued with ideas, imagination and feelings. Code expresses our thoughts made digital: a medium for our values, cultures and ways of life.

In other words, we are expressing ourselves when we write code. Our code reflects who we are. It reflects where we come from. It reflects how we want to be in the world.

If there's one thing I hope we coders learn from the best of the musical world, it's compassion, tolerance and respect for freedom of expression in all the ways music represents.

In a recent interview the Chinese American cellist Yo Yo Ma, explains,

"The job of a musician is to transcend technique in order to express something of value. We explore lots of different music through our instruments and we love it because it allows us to encounter different states of mind."

As developers, as we write code, we're trying to express something of value. We're exploring and interacting with our world through code and the applications we write.

But what does he mean by transcending technique?

At the start we examined lots of different skills, aspects of knowledge and ways to pay attention to the world. The sorts of things we learn about when we become musicians or coders.

I think these are what YoYo Ma means by techniques to be transcended.

When we express ourselves, we take all this stuff, assemble it, refine it, fix it, break it and play around with it...

In so doing, we also put something of ourselves into the thing we're making.

Only when we feel safe and secure do we send it into the world so others experience it.

I get to encounter a little bit of you in this thing you have created, and my world is made larger as a result.

To finish, I'm going to attempt to transcend my really shonky piano technique, and play you a final piece of music.

While I play, consider the ways in which you may transcend programming technique and create something that expresses your unique and valuable place in our world.
