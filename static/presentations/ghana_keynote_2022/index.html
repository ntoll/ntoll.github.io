Before I start, a little about myself...

Me in my natural habitat...

My original calling is as a classically trained musician.

I became a teacher... philosophy of education.

...but ended up in computing. How..?

Story of BBC micro.

David Allen, the project's producer, explained that, "[t]he aim was to democratise computing. We didn't want people to be controlled by it, but to control it."

That feeling of empowerment... I can make cool stuff happen.

In a recent government report, the BBC micro and other school based computing efforts were cited as a direct influence of the strength of the UK's software engineering industry.

Is there something similar today..?

Yes... micro:bit.

  • A simple device to learn to code.
  • A million devices delivered... 1 to every 11yo in the UK.
  • PSF was a partner... it runs MicroPython.
  • Let's see what it can do.

This is just the same as giving out free musical instruments to students.

Can you imagine how she sounds..?

What do you think a beginner programmer would feel like?

Folks need guidance. That means:

  • Educational resources
  • Mentors
  • Opportunities to explore

Some of the resources online are too good to be true.

It's not a race.

In fact, this sort of attitude is deeply harmful to learners.

I'm not the only one to think so, either.

Learning to code isn't just technical stuff.

Learning to code is a cultural activity.

I want to honour the work done already here in Ghana.

  • Workshops for PyData
  • PyClubs at universities
  • PyScholars at school level

These efforts are not alone. You have friends, allies and collaborators all over the world.

Over a decade ago, I was involved in founding the education track at PyCon UK. Sadly, it is now defunct.

As a community we wanted to...

...inspire in our newest Pythonistas.

We wanted them to see this is a long, slow...

...and at times frustrating journey.

But it's a journey made possible because folks (no matter their age or level of coding) were encouraged to step up and support learners.

By taking these steps learners encountered and practiced what it is to be a Python coder...

...in an inclusive, supportive and diverse community.

Between 2012-2015, each year, we welcomed up to 50 teachers and over 100 kids to PyConUK.

We encouraged folks to sustain their learning journey, build on from what they had encountered at the conference, and step into our community.

But we can do more.

I'm a lazy engineer...

What do other disciplines do to encourage the acquisition of technical skill in a sustained way that supports the learner while encouraging them to become aware of the culture into which they are joining.

Martial arts, music, ballet, life-guards, and so on...

Stepping stones.

Deliberate practice of the real thing...

A place where folks have a learning journey mapped out.

A place where folks get mentorship.

A place where folks achievements are celebrated.

  • Learn by doing things programmers do
  • Community education (not self educated)
  • Focus on creativity, imagination AND technical skill.
Code runs our world.

Yet code isn't written for computers.

We write code because we are human beings
  imbued with ideas, imagination and feelings.
Code expresses our thoughts made digital:
  a medium for our values, cultures and ways of life.

If you knew code, what would you create?

CodeGrades are to code,
  as belts are to martial arts:
Steps on a journey to experience.

Learn and level up with coding projects,
  the higher the grade, the greater the challenge.

We help you:
  learn coding with highly-skilled programmers,
  build a portfolio to show your progress, and,
  master the art of coding on a journey of personal and technical discovery.

We'll be with you for every step...

Because those who venture into the world of code
  will be the ones inventing our future.

What will your contribution be..?

The only way to find out, is to try.

Education makes our future possible.

What that future is depends upon who turns up to provide such education.

Don't just sit there, do something!
