The story of how MicroPython came to the micro:bit

  • MicroPython started as a side project
  • Extraordinarily successful Kickstarter
  • Full reimplementation of Python 3 for microcontrollers
  • PyBoard, ESP8266, European Space Agency
  • Flourishing community
  • Testament to Damien's talent and determination

In addition to the runtime, the BBC wanted a web-based editor. We wrote one, but discovered that we needed to build a whole eco-system.

  • We need a cross platform native editor
  • We need tools for the command line
  • We need projects to inspire others
  • We need a website for Python in education to spread the word

So we got to work (around 60-80 different developers from as young as 11 have been involved in the project to a greater or lesser extent).

http://codewith.mu/

An editor for kids, created from the feedback received by the Raspberry Pi Foundation. Currently only micro:bit specific, but generic version coming soon.

Designed to take ZERO effort to use.

What has education got to do with you..?

  • Part of our job as developers is to teach / mentor each other.
  • To describe programming so kids understand you indicates you know your craft at a deep level:
  • You know what to leave in and appreciate what should be left out of an explanation.
  • You have clear enough mental models of the concepts of programming that you can accurately analogize and summarise.
  • By explaining yourself in simple and easy to understand languages demonstrates your own clarity of thought.
  • Finally, it is unambiguously good to put something back into the wider Python community.
